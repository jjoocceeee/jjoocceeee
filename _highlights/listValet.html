ListValet

ListValet is a new startup which connects people who are good at selling things online, to people who have items to sell. Following that Uber Model, except for online sellers. When I was first introduced to the company, not only did I just fall in love with the founders and the idea, I knew it would be the perfect opportunity to learn from a small team and get to be involved in every part of such a large and complex technology infrastructure! The team was just about to begin rebuilding the entire portfolio from scratch, so I jumped on, and was involved with it all.
Tech Stack:

  • Back-end: GraphQL, node.js
  • Database: MongoDB
  • Front-end: React.js, Bootstrap
  • Infrastrucutre: AWS, Kubernetes
  • Plugins: Twilio, Dwolla, SendGrid, SmartyStreets, Google Captcha, etc.

Willing to work hard and learn, the team let me be involved in all aspects of web platform. I was able to design database schemas, contribute to the GraphQL API, and brought to life the front-end.
My main tasks included adding functionality and implementing designs for the front end, and adding GraphQL resolvers to the API. I spent my time 70% on the front end, and 20% on the backend, and 10% on Infrastructure. I built about 75% of the frontend user interface.

React.js

Having never worked with React before, I had to go up a huge learning curve to begin contributing. I am now in love with React. React solves so many problems that front-end developers face. Creating reusable components, and compartmentalizing code, allows front-end development ability to create things much faster, and more efficiently. Being able to add state to a component, allows for more complex components to be built with React.
